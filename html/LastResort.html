What was the project?

This is the project I worked on for my third year of University. It is an online Co-op base defense shooter.

I worked on it for a full year in a big team of 29 People: 2 Producers, 1 Audio Designer, 4 Game Designers, 4 Level Designers, 2 Animators, 2 Riggers, 1 Prop Artist, 4 Character/Environment Artists, 2 Network Programmers, 1 Physics Programmer, 1 Generalist Programmer and 3 AI Programmer

We created the game in Unreal Engine using Blueprints and C++. My responsibilities were AI Programming.

The game is set to release on Steam, first in Early Access and then by the end of the academic year as a full release. You can see the Steam Page here.

What did I learn?

This project has taught me a lot about working in a big team. It was the biggest team I have worked in yet and also the first time I had to share my own responsibilties with other programmers. I think there were a lot of things we could have done better. The biggest one of that is planning with a Player Perspective in mind. A lot of development was done without considering the actual gameplay and I think it was the biggest pitfall of the project.

HTN

The original plan for the AI was to create an HTN Planner in Unreal Engine for the AI. HTN stands for Hierarchical Task Network and it's a system used for example in Killzone. It offers fast and organic feeling AI Actions. However, it turned out that it was out of scope for us to create such a system especially because we also wanted an Editor interface for it, so that AI Designers can make the AI Behavior as they intend.

As a contingency plan, we went with Unreal Engines Behavior Trees coupled with a Utilty-Action System. This system led to a lot of bugs and inconviences when creating the AI and I would not choose to do it like this again.

Being a Scrum Master

In the span of this project, I volunteered to be a Scrum Master. I wanted to try out a role with more responsibility in terms of planning and leading a team. I learned about communicating effectively with the Producers and mediating their requests to my feature team.

In the end, I have to admit that I did not enjoy being a Scrum Master. I am not a fan of the responsibility and I found the Sprint Planning Sessions extremely exhausting. That being said, I am glad that I tried it and I think that if I had to, I could be a Scrum Master again.
